## I. CHAPTER 3 ADDITIONAL RULES - Coverage & Liability

### Multi-Vehicle Accident Liability (§33)
- **Rule**: When 2+ vehicles involved in accident, each vehicle's insurer liable only for damage caused by their vehicle
- **Impact**: Prevents one insurer from bearing costs of other vehicles' liability
- **Key Details**: Each insurer pays their own vehicle's portion

### Rented E-Scooter Driver Injury (§34a) - NEW VEHICLE TYPE
- **Rule**: For rented e-scooter accidents, ONLY medical costs compensated (not property damage to scooter itself)
- **Impact**: Limited coverage specifically for rented e-scooter users
- **Key Details**: Personal injury fully covered; property damage to scooter excluded

### Workers' Compensation Coordination (§36)
- **Rule**: Traffic insurance compensations coordinated with workers' accident insurance to avoid double compensation
- **Impact**: Reduces traffic insurance payout when workers' comp already covers
- **Calculation**: Traffic compensation reduced by workers' comp amount received

### Good Samaritan Damages (§39)
- **Rule**: Person assisting accident victim can claim compensation for damages they incur
- **Impact**: Encourages helping accident victims without fear of uncompensated loss
- **Example**: Damages from helping rescue victim, property damage while assisting

### Vehicle Combinations (§39a)
- **Rule**: In legal vehicle combinations (tractor+trailer), both vehicles covered if combination properly assembled
- **Impact**: Ensures tractor-trailer combinations have full coverage
- **Condition**: Must be legal combination; if combination improperly connected, coverage may be affected

### Theft After Policy Ends (§41)
- **Rule**: Vehicle stolen after policy ended can claim compensation if theft reported AND policy ended within 30 days
- **Impact**: Grace period protection for recently terminated policies
- **Requirement**: Theft must be reported to police

### Competition/Racing Exclusion (§41a)
- **Rule**: Driver injured in competition or racing cannot claim from own insurance
- **Impact**: Excludes motorsport/racing activities from coverage
- **Exception**: Third-party victims still covered; only own driver excluded

### Insurer Liability Sharing (§51-52)
- **Rule 51**: Between multiple insurers, each covers their own vehicle's liability
- **Rule 52**: Coordination with railway liability act when both road vehicle and train involved
- **Impact**: Clear allocation of responsibility between road and rail

## II. CHAPTER 4 - MEDICAL CARE REIMBURSEMENT (§53-59) - FULLY NEW CATEGORY

### Medical Care Reimbursement Conditions (§53)
- **Core Principle**: Medical care must be appropriate, necessary, and follow care guidelines
- **Verification Required**: Insurer verifies appropriateness and necessity
- **Guidelines**: Must comply with established medical care guidelines

### Public Healthcare Reimbursement (§54)
- **Basis**: Follows actual costs OR agreed pricing between insurer and healthcare provider
- **Process**: Direct notification from public healthcare to insurer
- **Amount**: Full reimbursement of actual costs

### Full Cost Payment Right (§55)
- **Rule**: Public healthcare authority has RIGHT to full cost payment (not subject to negotiations)
- **Impact**: Insurers must pay full costs for public healthcare
- **Mandatory**: Cannot negotiate down public healthcare costs

### Healthcare Notification Duty (§56)
- **Requirement**: Public healthcare unit MUST notify insurer of treatment
- **Timeline**: Must notify within required timeframe
- **Impact**: Ensures insurer awareness of treatment

### Insurer Right to Direct Care (§57)
- **Rule**: Insurance company CAN direct injured party to specific treatment facility
- **Limitations**: Can only direct if medically appropriate
- **Purpose**: Cost control through facility selection

### Private Healthcare Without Commitment (§58)
- **Basis**: Reimbursement follows KELA (National Insurance Institute) pricing
- **Amount**: Capped at standard KELA rates
- **Condition**: No payment commitment from facility

### Private Healthcare With Commitment (§59)
- **Rule**: With payment commitment, full costs reimbursed
- **Amount**: 100% reimbursement of agreed costs
- **Requirement**: Prior commitment from facility

## III. CHAPTER 5 ADDITIONAL RULES - Claims Procedure & Liability

### Data Protection Limitation (§62a) - NEW
- **Rule**: Data subject rights may be LIMITED for claim processing
- **Basis**: GDPR (EU 2016/679) allows restrictions for insurance purposes
- **Impact**: Insurers can limit victim's access requests during claim processing
- **Balance**: Must still honor essential requests

### Claimant Notification Duty (§68)
- **Rule**: Claimant MUST notify insurer of status changes affecting compensation
- **Examples**: Employment changes, recovery status, other income received
- **Consequence**: Failure to notify may reduce compensation

### Foreign Claims Representative (§69)
- **Rule**: For foreign insurers, Finland MUST have appointed claims representative
- **Function**: Represents foreign insurer for Finnish claims
- **Accessibility**: Must be locally accessible to claimants

### Right to Claim From Representative (§70)
- **Rule**: Victim can claim compensation directly from Finnish claims representative
- **Impact**: No need to pursue foreign insurer directly
- **Process**: Representative acts as insurer proxy

### Centre Liability for Delay (§71)
- **Rule**: Traffic Insurance Centre LIABLE for damages if claim handling delayed
- **Impact**: Centre must pay compensation for processing delays
- **Standard**: Claims must be handled within statutory timeframes

### Victim Information Right (§72)
- **Rule**: Victim has RIGHT to information from Traffic Insurance Centre
- **Types**: Claim status, decision reasons, appeal procedures
- **Timeline**: Information provided without unreasonable delay

### Subrogation Rights (§73-74)
- **§73 Insurer Subrogation**: Insurance company can recover paid compensation from responsible third party
- **§74 Centre Subrogation**: Centre can recover uninsured/unknown vehicle damages from liable party
- **Purpose**: Cost recovery mechanism

## IV. CHAPTER 6 - POOL SYSTEM (§75-78) - FULLY NEW CATEGORY

### Pool System Purpose & Costs (§75)
- **Function**: Shared system where insurers cover Centre's costs collectively
- **Costs Covered**: 
  - Centre operations
  - Uninsured vehicle damages
  - Unknown vehicle damages
  - Administrative expenses
- **Mechanism**: All insurers contribute proportionally

### Pool Contribution Calculation (§76)
- **Basis**: Market share + claims history
- **Formula**: Contribution = (Market Share %) × (Claims History Factor)
- **Fairness**: Those with worse claims history contribute more
- **Annual**: Recalculated yearly based on performance

### Pool Contribution Payment (§77)
- **Requirement**: Mandatory payment within specified timeline
- **Enforcement**: Non-payment has consequences
- **Deadline**: Payment due by regulatory deadline
- **Amount**: Determined by calculation formula

### Portfolio Transfer Effect (§78)
- **Rule**: When insurer transfers insurance portfolio, contributions adjusted
- **Impact**: Transferring company's liability changes
- **New Company**: Takes on transferred portfolio's contribution share
- **Timing**: Adjustments made at transfer date

## V. CHAPTER 7 ADDITIONAL RULES - Information & Administration

### Authority Appeal Right (§81)
- **Rule**: Healthcare authorities have right to appeal insurance decisions
- **Example**: If denied reimbursement for public healthcare costs
- **Process**: Appeal through appropriate court/administrative body

### Information Access Rights (§82)
- **Rule**: Insurer and Centre have right to obtain necessary information from authorities
- **Source**: Healthcare records, police reports, social services
- **Purpose**: Verify claims and liability

### Information Sharing Right (§84)
- **Rule**: Insurance company can share information with Centre and other stakeholders
- **Limitations**: Only for claim processing, regulatory, or legal purposes
- **Privacy**: Must comply with data protection law

### Information Centre Duties (§86)
- **Function**: Centre maintains vehicle and insurance registers
- **Responsibility**: Accurate record-keeping for all vehicles
- **Purpose**: Track insurance status and coverage

### Centre Additional Duties (§87)
- **Functions**: 
  - International cooperation (EU, EEA coordination)
  - Green card system administration
  - Data exchange with other countries
  - Bureau of claims coordination
  - Guarantee fund management

### Data Submission to Centre (§88)
- **Requirement**: Insurers MUST submit required data to Centre
- **Data Types**: Vehicle info, insurance status, claims history
- **Timeline**: Submission within regulatory deadlines

### Statistical Research (§89)
- **Authority**: Financial Supervisory Authority conducts research
- **Purpose**: Market analysis, statistics, trend identification
- **Requirement**: Insurers must provide data

### Trafi Notifications (§90)
- **Requirement**: Transport Agency must notify Centre of vehicle registrations
- **Data**: New registrations, cancellations, transfers
- **Purpose**: Keep Centre registers current

### Customs Duties (§91)
- **Requirement**: Customs must notify Centre of imported vehicles
- **Data**: Vehicle identification, importer info
- **Purpose**: Ensure imported vehicles get proper insurance

### Official Liability (§95)
- **Rule**: Officials liable for damages from wrongful/negligent decisions
- **Impact**: Accountability for Centre and insurance company personnel
- **Example**: Wrongful denial of compensation, improper delay

## VI. INSOLVENCY CHAPTER - Special Procedures

### Centre Covers Company Insolvency (§91a)
- **Rule**: If insurer becomes INSOLVENT, Centre compensates victims
- **Scope**: Full compensation to victims despite insurer failure
- **Requirement**: Victim must be Finnish resident
- **Critical**: Ensures compensation despite company failure

### Insolvency Handling Procedures (§91b)
- **Rule**: Special procedures for handling insolvent insurer
- **Roles**: Centre acts as insolvency administrator
- **Duties**: Claims distribution, asset recovery
- **Timeline**: Expedited processing for urgent claims

### Company Winding Up (§92)
- **Requirement**: Procedures for winding up insurer with traffic portfolio
- **Protection**: Continuing coverage during wind-up
- **Duration**: Wind-up completion within regulatory period

### Additional Payment Obligation (§93)
- **Rule**: Policyholders may have ADDITIONAL payment obligation if company fails
- **Amount**: Up to premium-equivalent amount
- **Purpose**: Ensure fund solvency if insurer fails

### Joint Guarantee Contribution (§94)
- **Rule**: Insurance companies may be liable for JOINT GUARANTEE FEE
- **Trigger**: When guarantee fund activated due to company failure
- **Amount**: Shared among remaining insurers
- **Purpose**: Collective protection mechanism

## VII. INTERNATIONAL COVERAGE - Additional Rules

### EEA Citizen Choice of Law (§13)
- **Rule**: Finnish resident can CHOOSE Finnish law for accident in other EEA countries
- **Benefit**: Victim chooses most favorable law
- **Condition**: Accident must be in EEA country
- **Impact**: Simplifies legal proceedings

### Foreign Vehicle in Finland (§13)
- **Rule**: Foreign vehicle accident in Finland = Finnish minimum coverage applies
- **Protection**: Victims protected by Finnish standards
- **Mandatory**: Automatic upgrade if foreign coverage insufficient

### Border Traffic Coverage (§7)
- **Limitation**: Border traffic insurance covers personal injury ONLY in Finland, Sweden, Norway
- **Property**: Property damage not covered in other countries
- **Cost**: Limits insurer exposure on short-term policies

### EEA Transit Protection (§13)
- **Rule**: During EEA transit through non-green-card countries, Finnish law applies
- **Route**: Direct transit between EEA countries
- **Protection**: Maintains coverage during necessary transit
